Police Shootings by Race

This bar chart groups the data to show the number of shootings by race. A bar chart was used so that the number of shootings by race could be easily compared. It would have been helpful to also incorporate the population proportion of each racial group; we see that in this dataset, there were a higher number of white people who died in a police shooting. However, given that they make up the majority of the population, we cannot conclude from this graph that white people are more likely to be shot and killed by a police officer.

Police Shootings by Month

This chart groups the number of police shootings that occurred in each month of 2015. A line chart was used to see if there were any trends in the number of police shootings throughout the year. Additional helpful information could have been if there were any significant events that happened during certain months that could impact a person's likelihood of getting killed by the police.

Police Shootings by Age

This chart is a histogram of the distribution of police shootings by age. A histogram was used to show the age groups most impacted by police shootings. It would have been more meaningful to interlay this information with gender, race, and socioeconomic status so that we could have a more intersectional, holistic picture.
